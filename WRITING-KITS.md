# Still Got It — Character Writing Kits

One kit per character. Paste the relevant kit (plus the shared rules below) into
the writing model together with the HANDOFF.md prompt template. The design brief
remains the source of truth for backstory; kits are the working distillation.

### Current prose guardrails

- Quips are rare. Most dialogue should answer the previous line, ask a practical question, dodge something, or change the subject like a human.
- Do not let every scene become quick clever repartee. Wit belongs to specific characters and specific moments, not the house style.
- Ban secular “spiritually”. Use it only when a scene is actually about spirituality, religion, ghosts, ritual, or faith.
- Avoid ornamental AI similes that cannot literally happen. If a phrase draws attention to itself before it reveals character, cut it.
- Noticeboard items must stay neutral. They may hint through objects, mood, and routine, but they must not reveal which resident will appear unless that resident publicly posted the event under their own name.

## Shared rules (paste with every kit)

Voice: plain narration, short paragraphs, dry Australian register. Characters
carry the wit; the narrator sets tables and opens doors. Emotional peaks get
plainer, not fancier. Objects do the symbolic work (a boa, a toolbox photo, a
second cup of tea). Silence and small actions beat speeches.

Banned: "Beat." / "a pause" as rhythm crutches (physical action instead);
narrated emotions ("she felt", "something in her", "a wave of"); paired-fragment
corrections more than once per scene ("Not X. Y."); "couldn't help but";
"a mix of X and Y"; twinkling eyes; voices barely above whispers; palpable
anything; soft chuckles; moral statements; theme announcements; choice text that
describes effects ("Reassure her kindly") instead of words/deeds ("You'll be
great. Everyone will love you."); em-dash chains; tidy uplifting scene buttons.

Every scene must pass: could a stranger tell which character is speaking with
the names removed? If not, rewrite until yes.

## RHONDA (route built — kit for extensions)

Belief: *If I am admired, I will be loved.* Truth: she needs a community, not an
audience. Register: theatrical, precise, deflects with wit, honest only after
two deflections minimum. Vain but never stupid; her self-awareness arrives late
in a line, quietly. Says "darling" to people she likes and people she is about
to destroy; identical tone. Emotional tells: goes plain, goes brief, touches
objects (script, programme, boa).
Open task (HANDOFF item 6): one week-2 scene, suggested spec — Rhonda directing
the seating of the lounge for someone's visiting grandchildren "so the light is
right", accidentally revealing she stages every room she is in; grants nothing
but +1 and texture.
Never: let her cry, monologue about fame, or thank anyone without an edge.

## BOB (crossroads built — kit for week-4 + duet)

Belief: *Strength means carrying everything yourself.* He can discuss Vietnam
matter-of-factly — mud, boredom, blokes, logistics. He cannot say June's name
in a sentence longer than four words until after the reunion. Post-reunion he
gains maybe 20% more words and one unprompted question per scene; that is the
entire visible change. Register: flat declaratives, tool vocabulary, kindness
via acts (sliding a screwdriver over, making room). Duet with Rhonda: she makes
him laugh against his will; he is the only person she doesn't perform for.
Week-4 consequence scene spec: success — Bob asks the player to check a poem
"for spelling" (it is not about spelling); failure — the good jacket goes to
the op-shop bag, then quietly comes back out of it.

## MIRANDA — arc to build

Belief: *If I do everything right, I will never feel helpless again.* Truth:
imperfect people are still worth trusting. Register: clipped, precise, zero
adjectives she doesn't need, thanks people by finding their work "acceptable."
Warmth appears as logistics (a spare pair of gloves in your size, unexplained).
Key memory (to author): a concrete image of the poverty years — suggested: she
mentions keeping the family's one good tablecloth mended for thirty years
because "you can be poor or you can look poor, and only one of those is
compulsory." Memory id suggestion: `miranda_good_tablecloth`.
Crossroads: garden competition (week 3). Base: she does everything herself,
wins, exhausted, thanks nobody. Pressure point: the day before judging,
too many jobs, her wrist or her pride failing; success choice gives her own
words back (the tablecloth logic: accepting help ≠ looking helpless). Success
consequence: the hall at the concert is decorated with flowers OTHER people
grew under her direction; people give her small practical gifts she pretends
not to like. Failure: the garden is perfect and she watches the concert from
the end of a row, half asleep.
Concert conditional blocks + 2 ending lines + resident-note ladder required.

## PABLO — arc to build

Belief: *Love means providing until there is nothing left to give.* Truth:
letting others care for him is closeness, not weakness. Register: warm,
food-first metaphors used practically not poetically, hospitality as reflex,
deflects care by feeding the carer. Grief present as habit: he still cooks for
six. Key memory (to author): the wife's signature dish and one detail of her
making it — she seasoned by taste, frowned at the pot, added a little more, and was always right. Memory id: `pablo_carmen_rice` (name the wife; brief says wife
died ten years ago, name not fixed — pick one and add to DESIGN.md).
Crossroads: harvest feast (week 3, day 20 evening). Base: he substitutes a
different dish; nobody notices; the recipe card stays in his wallet. Pressure
point: afternoon of the feast, the choice of what to cook; success = the dish
cooked WITH help he accepts. Success consequence: someone else cooks for him in
week 4 and he sits down and lets them. Failure: the card stays in the wallet;
he tops up everyone's plates standing up, like always.
Duet with Miranda continues regardless (data-duets.js).

## JEAN — arc to build

Belief: *The next cause matters more than my own happiness.* Truth: joy does
not need to be earned through usefulness. Register: loose, funny, lived-in;
rhetoric arrives mid-sentence then gets laughed at by its own speaker; Rae
(daughter, weekly visits) is her soft spot and her mirror. Key memory (to
author): a moment she was purely happy that achieved nothing — suggested:
three days at a festival in 1971 she has never told anyone about because
"nothing came of it," which is the point. Memory id: `jean_festival_days`.
Crossroads: fig tree campaign (week 3). Base: she runs everything, the campaign
fizzles into paperwork, she jokes it off and goes quiet. Pressure point:
delegation — success choice hands the campaign to the young council officer or
to Rae's generation, using her own memory back at her (a thing can matter
without her carrying it). Success consequence: she attends the concert as an
audience member with nothing to organise, and enjoys it — hardest scene in the
game to write plainly; resist all temptation to have her say so.

Jean canon update: retired librarian and freelance writer. She is not a choir character. Her concert contribution is a spoken-word/slam-poetry piece. On success, she performs it with confidence; on failure, she still performs, but rushes the ending and hides behind a joke before the room can fully receive it. The failure state is weaker, not humiliating.

## AL — arc to build

Belief: *If I stop being charming, nobody will choose to spend time with me.*
Truth: people like him most when he forgets to perform. Register: quick, flirty,
self-aware enough to wink at his own act; the act NEVER fully drops — success
is the act getting 10% quieter, not a confession. Loneliness is allowed one
sentence per scene, maximum, and he must immediately cap it with a joke. Key
memory (to author): one true thing under the band stories — suggested: he was
the one who drove everyone home after gigs, sober, because he liked being
needed at 3am better than being adored at 10pm. Memory id: `al_designated_driver`.
Crossroads: dance night (week 3). Base: he works the room, charms everyone,
goes home alone with the act intact. Pressure point: gated scene where the
music is too fast for his hip and the choice is to perform through it or sit
one out; success choice uses the memory (being present beats being impressive).
Success consequence: at the concert he sings the song properly instead of
mugging through it, and dedicates it to nobody, which everyone notices.
Duet with Jean: she heckles; he escalates; they are the only two people in the
village neither of whom is fooled.
